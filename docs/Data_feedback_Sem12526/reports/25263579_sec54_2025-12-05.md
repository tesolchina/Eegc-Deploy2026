Employers frequently substitute automated systems for low-skilled roles because automation reduces labour costs and error rates, which leads to layoffs in factories and administrative centres. These dislocations are particularly severe in communities that depend on a narrow range of industries, producing significant declines in local employment and income. Nevertheless, such short-term losses can be mitigated: historical experience shows that technological change often creates new employment opportunities if displaced workers receive targeted retraining and institutional support.

Moreover, AI can help fill labour gaps. In ageing societies, it can take over caregiving drudgery, freeing humans for emotional and complex healthcare tasks. Furthermore, operating and maintaining AI systems requires ongoing workforce input, creating new roles in supervision and system management.

AI spurs the growth of entirely new industries, such as machine learning development, data analytics, and other AI-enabled services. To maximise these benefits, proactive measures are crucial.
